Wordly

Project

​Wordly is a mobile app designed to empower people to learn new vocabulary by examining the experience​ ​of​ ​memorizing​ ​and​ ​understanding​ ​new concepts,​ ​techniques,​ ​and​ ​terms​.

Year

2018

UX Methods

Competitive & User Research, User Needs & Persona, User Flows, Wireframing & Prototyping, Usability Testing

Tools

Realtime Board, Prott, Lookback, Figma

Competitive Analysis

​After conducting an in depth analysis of Duolingo, Memrise, Babbel and iLingual I was able to identify some key pros and cons, as well as gaps in the market that Wordly could potentially fill.

Pros

Study goals & reminders, caters to different learning methods, offline use

Cons
Lack of onboarding, cannot add own vocabulary words

User Interviews

In order to meet users’ needs, it is important to understand how they see products currently available and determine how we can provide them with the features they may be missing.

NEED A WAY TO ASK QUESTIONS

Users find learning on apps difficult because they cannot ask why their answer is wrong. There is no way to get clarification.

“It’s hard using an app because no one explains the logic behind the answer”

RESCHEDULE INCONVENIENT REMINDERS

Users with irregular schedules find it difficult to set a recurring alarm because they never know if they’ll be free at the time or not. They would benefit from a snooze or reset feature.

“I ended up ignoring reminders that went off at inconvenient times”

Proto Persona

Problem

Maria needs a way to understand when she gets an answer wrong because she feels that explanations prevent her from making the same mistake.

Hypothesis

We believe that by creating a feature that explains why an answer is incorrect for Maria, we will help increase Maria’s understanding of her new language.
